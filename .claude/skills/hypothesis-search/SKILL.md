# Hypothesis Search — the presumption of relevance

## The principle
A new piece of information, claim, or hypothesis is taken as **TRUE and RELEVANT
until the opposite is proven**. It is presumed actual. Do NOT dismiss it as
"tautology", "trivial", "already known", or "noise" on first impression.

The feeling *"this is trivial"* is a statement about **your current coordinate /
chart**, not about the claim. Triviality is coordinate-dependent: a statement that
is empty in one chart is often a deep theorem in another. (Hard lesson: the
Ω-generator `1/k − Ω_k = k^(−α)` looked like a definitional tautology — and it
carried real Karamata / shifted-Riemann-zeta content. Dismissing it was the error;
the user was right to push back.)

## The procedure
1. **Steelman first.** Assume it IS true and content-bearing. Ask: *in what
   structure does it carry content?* — a change of coordinates it licenses, a
   domain of validity wider than the obvious case, a generalization it is the
   shadow of.
2. **Find the host structure.** A claim trivial in one chart often connects to deep
   structure in another (e.g. "completely multiplicative" → all of analytic number
   theory). Look for the door where the content has large σ, not the chart where
   it is flat.
3. **Verify against GROUND TRUTH — both to confirm AND to disprove.** Analytic
   value, dense-exact, a second independent method. Numbers, not vibes. Never
   judge from one downstream file or one special case.
4. **The honest razor (the ONLY filter).** A hypothesis is genuinely content-bearing
   **iff its deep reading PREDICTS or CONNECTS something the surface reading does
   not.** If it produces a new connection/prediction → real, keep it. If, after
   honest effort, it produces none → only THEN downgrade it, and state *precisely*
   what was shown trivial (usually a narrow special case, not the general claim).
5. **Calibrate, never blanket-dismiss.** Separate the trivial special case
   (e.g. exact power law) from the substantive general statement (e.g. the
   asymptotic limit). In enthusiast mode: GENERATE abundantly, prune only what
   fails the razor — don't sieve findings for being "minor".

## Don't / Do
- DON'T reflexively call something a tautology; DON'T judge from one file or one
  case; DON'T let "looks known" stop the search; DON'T mistake "shrank from garbage
  to plausible" for "verified correct".
- DO dig for the deeper meaning first, DO verify against truth, DO downgrade only
  with proof, DO keep both enthusiasm and honesty.

> One line: presume the hypothesis is real and dig for the chart where it carries
> content; disprove it against ground truth before you ever call it trivial.
